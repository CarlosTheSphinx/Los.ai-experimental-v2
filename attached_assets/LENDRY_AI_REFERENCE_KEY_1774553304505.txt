SECTION 1: LOAN TYPES (STANDARDIZED CATEGORIES)
================================================================================

The "Loan Type" column indicates the types of financing a lender provides.
Each lender may offer MULTIPLE loan types (separated by semicolons).

1. BRIDGE
   Definition: Short-term financing that "bridges" the gap between closing on 
               one property and selling another, or between acquisition and 
               permanent financing
   Use Case: Interim financing during real estate transitions
   Typical Duration: 6-24 months
   Related Property Types: Any (Commercial, Residential, Mixed Use, etc.)
   AI Matching Note: Usually for time-sensitive deals needing quick capital
   
2. CONSTRUCTION
   Definition: Financing for ground-up development, renovations, or significant 
               improvements to properties
   Use Case: New construction projects, major rehabs, fix-and-flip projects
   Typical Duration: Duration of construction phase + holdover
   Related Property Types: Multifamily, Office, Retail, Industrial, Hospitality
   AI Matching Note: Requires detailed construction plans and timeline
   
3. DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio)
   Definition: Financing based on the property's Net Operating Income (NOI) 
               rather than borrower's personal income
   Use Case: Investment properties with existing income streams
   Requirements: NOI must be 1.0x+ or higher (varies by lender)
   Related Property Types: Multifamily, Office, Retail, Industrial
   AI Matching Note: Asset-based lending; good for investor-owned properties
   
4. A&D (Acquisition & Development)
   Definition: Financing for land acquisition and infrastructure development 
               before vertical construction
   Use Case: Land development, site preparation, entitlement projects
   Typical Duration: 12-36 months
   Related Property Types: Land, Development, Residential Development
   AI Matching Note: Focuses on site control, entitlements, and master planning
   
5. FIX & FLIP
   Definition: Short-term financing for purchase and renovation of single-family 
               or small multi-unit properties for resale
   Use Case: Residential real estate investing/rehabbing
   Typical Duration: 6-18 months
   Related Property Types: Residential
   AI Matching Note: Exit strategy is critical; based on after-repair value (ARV)
   
6. LONG-TERM FINANCING
   Definition: Permanent, traditional mortgage financing (agency or portfolio)
   Subtypes: Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac, FHA, CMBS, portfolio mortgages
   Use Case: Stabilized, income-producing properties
   Typical Duration: 5-30 years
   Related Property Types: Residential, Multifamily, Office, Retail, Industrial
   AI Matching Note: Strictest underwriting; best rates; longer approval timeline
   
7. LAND DEVELOPMENT
   Definition: Financing for raw land or development projects including 
               infrastructure, entitlements, and vertical construction
   Use Case: Master-planned communities, lot development, site development
   Typical Duration: 18-48 months (varies greatly)
   Related Property Types: Land, Development, Residential, Commercial
   AI Matching Note: Highest risk category; requires detailed feasibility study

SECTION 2: PROPERTY TYPES (STANDARDIZED CATEGORIES)
================================================================================

The "Property Types" column indicates what types of real estate a lender focuses on.
Each lender may specialize in MULTIPLE property types (separated by semicolons).

1. RESIDENTIAL
   Definition: Single-family homes, townhomes, condominiums (owner-occupied 
               or investment)
   Examples: Houses, single-family rentals, small residential development
   Financing Types Often Available: Long-term financing, Fix & Flip, DSCR
   AI Matching Note: May distinguish between owner-occupied vs. investment
   
2. MULTIFAMILY
   Definition: 2-unit to high-rise apartment buildings
   Examples: Duplexes, triplexes, garden apartments, mid-rise, high-rise
   Unit Range: 2+ units
   Financing Types Often Available: Construction, Long-term, DSCR, Bridge
   AI Matching Note: Most lenders have strong multifamily products
   
3. OFFICE
   Definition: Commercial office buildings (Class A, B, C)
   Examples: Office towers, suburban office parks, flex office
   Financing Types Often Available: DSCR, Long-term, Bridge, Construction
   AI Matching Note: Post-pandemic; supply/demand varies by market
   
4. RETAIL
   Definition: Ground-floor commercial space for retail, food, entertainment
   Examples: Shopping centers, standalone retail, strip malls, lifestyle centers
   Financing Types Often Available: DSCR, Long-term, Bridge
   AI Matching Note: Tenant quality and lease strength critical
   
5. INDUSTRIAL
   Definition: Warehouse, distribution, manufacturing, flex space
   Examples: Fulfillment centers, data centers, light manufacturing, logistics
   Financing Types Often Available: DSCR, Long-term, Bridge, Construction
   AI Matching Note: Strong demand; quality tenants drive financing approval
   
6. LAND
   Definition: Raw, undeveloped, or development-ready land
   Examples: Vacant land, entitled land, master-planned communities, lot banking
   Financing Types Often Available: A&D, Land Development, Bridge
   AI Matching Note: Highest risk; requires clear path to development
   
7. DEVELOPMENT
   Definition: Properties under development or redevelopment phase
   Examples: Vertical construction, master-planned communities, major renovations
   Financing Types Often Available: Construction, Land Development, A&D
   AI Matching Note: Focused on execution risk and developer experience
   
8. MIXED USE
   Definition: Properties combining multiple use types in one structure
   Examples: Retail + Residential, Office + Retail, Hotel + Residential
   Financing Types Often Available: Construction, Bridge, DSCR, Long-term
   AI Matching Note: More complex underwriting; requires detailed pro forma
   
9. HOSPITALITY
   Definition: Hotels, motels, resorts, and short-term rental properties
   Examples: Full-service hotels, select-service, boutique, Airbnb portfolios
   Financing Types Often Available: DSCR, Long-term, Bridge, Construction
   AI Matching Note: Highly volatile segment; occupancy and rate critical
   
10. STUDENT HOUSING
    Definition: Purpose-built or adapted housing near universities
    Examples: On-campus facilities, off-campus student apartments
    Financing Types Often Available: DSCR, Long-term, Construction
    AI Matching Note: Demographic-sensitive; school strength matters
    
11. SELF-STORAGE
    Definition: Climate-controlled or open storage facilities
    Examples: Mini storage, climate-controlled storage, car storage
    Financing Types Often Available: DSCR, Long-term, Bridge, Construction
    AI Matching Note: Stable, defensive asset class; strong operating metrics

SECTION 3: LOAN AMOUNT FORMATS
================================================================================

All loan amounts are standardized to one of these formats:

FORMAT 1: Range Format ($XM-$YM)
Example: $1M-$10M
Meaning: Lender will finance deals from $1 million to $10 million
AI Matching: Use this range to filter eligible lenders for deal size

FORMAT 2: Less Than (<$XM)
Example: <$5M
Meaning: Lender specializes in smaller deals, up to $5 million maximum
AI Matching: Indicates lender's sweet spot is in the small deal market

FORMAT 3: Up To (Up to $XM)
Example: Up to $30M
Meaning: Lender can do deals up to the specified amount
AI Matching: Indicates maximum but not minimum; may do smaller deals

================================================================================
SECTION 4: LOAN AMOUNT CONVERSION REFERENCE
================================================================================

M  = Million (1M = $1,000,000)
K  = Thousand (1K = $1,000)
MM = Million (same as M)

Examples for AI:
- $0.5M = $500,000
- $1M = $1,000,000
- $25M = $25,000,000
- $100M = $100,000,000

SECTION 5: AI MATCHING ALGORITHM INPUTS
================================================================================

When the Lendry AI evaluates a deal against the lender list:

REQUIRED INPUTS (from deal data):
1. Loan Amount: $X (must fall within lender's range)
2. Loan Type: Which type of financing is needed (Bridge, Construction, DSCR, etc.)
3. Property Type: What type of property (Residential, Office, Retail, etc.)
4. Geographic Region: Which region the deal is in

MATCHING LOGIC:
✓ MATCH: Loan amount falls within lender's range
✓ MATCH: Loan type appears in lender's list
✓ MATCH: Property type appears in lender's list
✓ MATCH: Geographic region matches lender's focus area

⚠ POTENTIAL MATCH: If property type is blank/missing (leave open for interpretation)
⚠ POTENTIAL MATCH: If loan type is blank/missing (may need contextual inference)
✗ NO MATCH: Deal size is outside lender's range
✗ NO MATCH: Loan type not offered by lender
✗ NO MATCH: Property type not served by lender
✗ NO MATCH: Geographic region excluded by lender

SECTION 8: DATA QUALITY NOTES FOR AI
================================================================================

BLANK FIELDS IN LOAN TYPE:
- Indicates data was not clearly specified in source material
- AI should NOT assume missing loan types mean "all types"
- AI should flag these as "need clarification" in confidence scoring
- May need manual research or direct lender contact

BLANK FIELDS IN PROPERTY TYPES:
- Indicates lender focus was not specified in source material
- May indicate generalist lenders or incomplete data capture
- AI should treat as "potentially broader than stated"
- Consider requesting additional information from lender

GENERIC "COMMERCIAL" PROPERTY TYPE:
- Some lenders only show "Commercial" without Office/Retail/Industrial breakdown
- AI should inquire with lender for property subtype focus
- Conservative approach: assume can do most commercial subtypes
- Aggressive approach: match any commercial deal to these lenders
